// MUMBAI FIELD GUIDE
Know before you land

Mumbai is one of the world's great cities. Dense, loud, beautiful, and deeply human. Here's what you need to know to navigate it well.

// CITY OVERVIEW
Mumbai at a glance

Mumbai is the financial capital of India — 21 million people on a narrow peninsula on the Arabian Sea. It's the home of Bollywood, the country's largest stock exchange, some of the world's most important spiritual traditions, and now, DEVCON VIII.

The city operates on its own logic. Traffic is chaotic but navigable. Food is extraordinary. People are warm. The gap between wealth and poverty is visible on every street. It's a city that overwhelms and then captivates.

November is one of the best times to visit — post-monsoon, 25–30°C, humidity dropping, and the city feels alive without being punishing.

// TRANSPORT
Getting around Mumbai
// Ola & Uber
App-based cabs
Work well. Always use the app — never negotiate at the airport exit. Surge pricing is common 8–10am and 6–9pm.
// Auto Rickshaws
Three-wheelers
Suburbs only (not South Mumbai). Great for short distances. Insist on the meter or use Ola Auto. Cheap and fast in narrow lanes.
// Local Train
The real Mumbai
Moves 8 million people daily. Overwhelming for first-timers. We don't recommend it unless you're adventurous and we're with you.

// DIWALI · FESTIVAL OF LIGHTS

The world's most spectacular festival of light — right after Devcon.

Diwali is India's most significant cultural celebration. Five days of lights, firecrackers, sweets, family gatherings, and spiritual significance. The victory of light over darkness.

In Mumbai it transforms the city — every building lit with diyas, streets filled with incense and sugar, fireworks visible from every rooftop. Unlike anything else in the world.

Most Devcon attendees will still be in or near India when it begins. Our Full Experience package includes a special group Diwali evening — real families, real celebrations, real Mumbai.

// EXPERIENCES
Mumbai beyond the conference
// Dharavi
The most misunderstood place in India
A self-organized city of 1 million people with a $1B economy in leather, pottery, and recycling. One of the most profound experiences in Mumbai. Context required before visiting.
// Marine Drive
The Queen's Necklace
A 3.6km promenade along the Arabian Sea. Best at sunset or after dark. One of the great urban walks on earth. 45 minutes from BKC by car.
// Crawford Market
Where old Mumbai lives
A Victorian-era market overflowing with spices, flowers, and produce. The architecture alone is worth the trip. Go early morning.
// Bandra
Where you want to be
Cafés, galleries, street art, colonial churches, the best bar scene in the city. Close to the Devcon venue. Where most attendees will spend their evenings.

// WHAT IS DEVCON?

Devcon is the annual developer conference organized by the Ethereum Foundation — the most significant gathering in the global Web3 ecosystem. Unlike consumer-facing crypto events, Devcon is where the builders are: core protocol researchers, application developers, infrastructure teams, and the thinkers who shape what the internet becomes.

Previous editions have been held in Berlin, Amsterdam, Cancún, Prague, Osaka, and Bogotá. Mumbai 2026 marks the first Devcon in India — a recognition of India's growing centrality in the global Web3 movement.

// INDIA'S WEB3 ECOSYSTEM

India has the second-largest developer population in the world and one of the fastest-growing crypto communities. Mumbai alone is home to dozens of serious Web3 companies, funds, and communities.
